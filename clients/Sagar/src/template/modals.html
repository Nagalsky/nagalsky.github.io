Case study: Reducing merchant acquisition and onboard costs for Ressy

Ressy is a location-based restaurant application that aggregates all local restaurant deals in real time by partnering with restaurants (merchants).

It then provides a platform for banks, credit card companies, and mobile wallets to provide significant discounts (up to 50% off) to their users.

In other words, it helps our clients offer sweet deals to their clients. But getting restaurants to partner with us took a lot of time and money. It’s hard enough to corral a busy owner, let alone convince them that

Initial merchant acquisition cost: ~$32

Initial time to on-board merchant: 5-7 days

My first step was to learn everything

I could about the current merchant acquisition flow, breaking down the time and cost for each step in our process:

  1. Cold calling
  2. Getting in touch with the restaurant owner
  3. First meeting
  4. Follow up meeting
  5. Negotiations
  6. Signing agreements
  7. Setting discounts
  8. Adding new deals to the system
  9. Going live with the new deals

It was quite a processWith that out of the way, it was time to crunch some numbers.

Armed with my analysis and research, I proposed a solution that would automate 80% of the current acquisition flow and lower costs by 53%.

Solution part one:

Partnering with another startup to acquire restaurant info

I nurtured a relationship with another startup that had all the information we could ever want about the restaurants we courted (things like owner names, contact details, opening hours, photos, menu, reviews, etc.).

They agreed to share this data with us via an API in exchange for licensing fees. No more cold calling - we could go straight to the owners with our best offers.

Before

Time: 1-3 days. Cost: $7–$8.

After

Time: 0 hrs. Cost: a few cents.

Solution part two:

Create an algorithm to assist sales executives

Initially, a sales executive had to negotiate custom deals with every restaurant. Owners were wary of us at first and there was lots of back-and-forth.

To expedite this process, I created an algorithm that fetches data for similar restaurants using the API our new partners provided.

Owners could see the % discount their direct competitors provided and make an informed decision much more easily. (I mean, who wants to be outdone by their competitors, right?)

Before

Time: 2-4 days. Cost: $20–$22.

After

Time: 1-2 days. Cost: $10–$12.

Solution part three:

Uploading restaurant data with the API.

Finally, I found another use for the API: expediting the onboarding process by pre-populating data in our app. We could upload details with the click of a button and avoid pesky human error.

Before

Time: 1 hour. Cost: $5.

After

Time: a few seconds. Cost: a few cents.

When all was said and done, the results made Ressy hum along much better than before.

Final merchant acquisition cost: ~$15 (a 53% improvement)

Final time to on-board: 3-4 days (a 42% improvement)

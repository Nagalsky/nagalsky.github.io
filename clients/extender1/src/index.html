What to avoid when buying WiFi extenders

Based on key performance considerations, here are five critical mistakes to avoid when purchasing a WiFi Extender:

Lack of wired backhaul support

WiFi Extenders that don’t support wired connections often suffer poor speed and reliability. Avoid relying solely on wireless repeaters that halve your bandwidth.

Poor placement

Placing the amplifier where the router’s signal is weak worsens performance. Always position it within strong coverage to extend, not replicate, a good signal.

Single-band amplifiers

Single-band devices struggle with congestion and slow speeds. Choose dual- or tri-band models to balance range and throughput efficiently.

Limited app control

Amplifiers without intuitive app control limit customization and easy troubleshooting. Look for devices with user-friendly management apps.

Low-quality antennas and radios

Cheap models with weak antennas or single radios will deliver poor range and inconsistent connections.

Avoid these pitfalls to ensure your WiFi Extender actually improves your home network instead of slowing it down.
